# Molecular Classification of Cancer

> [!ABSTRACT] Summary
> Molecular classification reveals that histologically similar cancers can have fundamentally different biology and treatment responses. Breast cancer has 4 intrinsic subtypes (Luminal A/B, HER2-enriched, Basal-like/TNBC) with dramatically different prognoses. Colorectal cancer has 4 consensus molecular subtypes (CMS1-4) with CMS4-mesenchymal being the worst. Gliomas are now classified by molecular features (IDH status, 1p/19q codeletion) rather than morphology alone (WHO 2021). The key insight: same histological grade can have vastly different outcomes based on molecular features.

## Notes

### 15.1 Breast Cancer Molecular Subtypes (Perou/Sørlie)

| Feature | Luminal A | Luminal B | HER2-Enriched | Basal-like (TNBC) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ER/PR** | ER+/PR+ | ER+/PR± | ER−/PR− | ER−/PR− |
| **HER2** | HER2− | HER2± | HER2+ | HER2− |
| **Ki-67** | Low (<14%) | High (≥14%) | Variable | High |
| **Grade** | Low | High | High | High |
| **Prognosis** | **Best** | Intermediate | Poor (pre-trastuzumab) | **Worst** |
| **Common mutations** | PIK3CA | PIK3CA | ERBB2 amp | TP53, BRCA1, RB1 loss |
| **H&E features** | Well-differentiated, tubules | Mod-poor differentiation | May have micropapillary | Poorly differentiated, solid sheets, high-grade nuclei |

#### TNBC Subtypes (Lehmann/Burstein Classification)

| Subtype | Key Features |
|---|---|
| **Basal-like 1 (BL1)** | DNA damage response, cell cycle genes |
| **Basal-like 2 (BL2)** | Growth factor pathways, metabolic |
| **Mesenchymal (M)** | EMT, motility, ECM interaction |
| **Mesenchymal stem-like (MSL)** | Similar to M + stem cell features |
| **Immunomodulatory (IM)** | Immune signaling — best prognosis |
| **Luminal androgen receptor (LAR)** | AR-driven, distinct biology |

### 15.2 Colorectal Cancer — Consensus Molecular Subtypes (CMS)

| Subtype | Frequency | Key Features | Prognosis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CMS1 (MSI-Immune)** | 14% | MSI-high, hypermutated, BRAF mutations, strong immune activation (cytotoxic T cells) | Best in early stage; **responds to immunotherapy** |
| **CMS2 (Canonical)** | 37% | CIN, WNT/MYC activation, epithelial, well-differentiated | Moderate |
| **CMS3 (Metabolic)** | 13% | KRAS mutations, metabolic dysregulation, mucinous features | Moderate |
| **CMS4 (Mesenchymal)** | 38% | TGF-β activation, stromal infiltration, EMT, CAF-rich, desmoplastic stroma, infiltrative borders | **Worst** |

> [!TIP] H&E Correlation
> CMS4 (Mesenchymal) shows the most distinct H&E features: prominent desmoplastic stroma, infiltrative borders, dense fibrotic reaction. CMS1 shows increased TILs. These are potentially classifiable by computational pathology from H&E alone.

### 15.3 Glioma — WHO 2021 Classification

The 2021 WHO classification represents a paradigm shift: **molecular features now define the diagnosis**, not just morphology.

| Type | Key Molecular Features | Prognosis |
|---|---|---|
| **IDH-wildtype glioblastoma** | TERT promoter mutation, EGFR amplification, +7/-10 copy number changes | Worst (~15 months median survival) |
| **IDH-mutant astrocytoma** | ATRX mutation, TP53 mutation, Grades 2–4 | Better (years to decades) |
| **IDH-mutant, 1p/19q-codeleted oligodendroglioma** | CIC/FUBP1 mutations, TERT promoter mutation | Best; sensitive to PCV chemotherapy |

> [!IMPORTANT] Key Insight
> Same histological grade can have **very different outcomes** based on molecular features. A Grade 3 IDH-mutant astrocytoma has a far better prognosis than a Grade 4 IDH-wildtype glioblastoma. Molecular testing is essential — morphology alone is insufficient.

## Summary

> [!TIP] Cornell Summary
> Molecular classification has transformed cancer diagnosis by revealing that histologically similar tumors have different biology. Breast cancer is divided into Luminal A (best prognosis), Luminal B, HER2-enriched, and Basal-like/TNBC (worst). CRC has 4 CMS subtypes: CMS1 (MSI, immunotherapy-responsive) through CMS4 (mesenchymal, worst prognosis). Gliomas are now classified primarily by IDH status and 1p/19q codeletion — same grade, different molecular subtype = vastly different outcomes. For computational pathology, the challenge is predicting molecular subtypes from H&E morphology alone — a major area of research.
